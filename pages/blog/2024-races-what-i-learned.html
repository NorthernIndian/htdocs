Race Season is Over. This is what I learned. (2024)

I just wrapped up the 2024 race season, and boy, it was a good one.

The season included the Whitefish Half Marathon, Footstock, Millaville Halfer, Powderface Halfer, and Lewiston Ultra's Moose 29k.

I started training on February 12 with one of Strava's 12-week McMillan Plans. I had a fresh pair of Brook Ghost 15s and a Vivo Activ 4 watch.

I had low expectations going into these races, because I performed poorly over the past several years. But this year, things started to click near the end of my training cycle and I performed above my expectations.

I set a new PR at the Whitefish Halfer and beat my previous best by 6 minutes. It was shocking and I found myself in a state of disbelief for about a week. I then placed 6th at the Footstock 10k and set a personal best for my 10k while being slightly hungover. Two weeks later, I beat my Whitefish PR by 2 minutes when I ran the Millaville Halfer. A couple weeks later I ran Powderface, my first trail race, and I finished 6th overall, 4th in the men's, and 1st in my category. Lastly, I ran the Moose 29k.
