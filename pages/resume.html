Foreword

This version of my resume is more than a list of jobs—it's a snapshot of my life experience. It includes both professional accomplishments and personal growth, offering a broader view of the work I’ve done and the values that drive me.

Why take this approach?

Over the years, I’ve seen companies repeatedly hire people who look good on paper but struggle in real life. Someone who mishandles personal finances is put in charge of a massive budget. Someone who can’t manage their own relationships ends up managing a team. Someone with no regard for health is tasked with overseeing health and safety.

These hiring decisions often come down to timing, charisma, or just plain luck. That’s life.

But if I were hiring, I’d ask different questions: Who is this person, really? What have they built—not just at work, but in life? Are they worth investing in?

This resume aims to answer those questions. It's a chance for anyone considering my skills to see the full picture.
