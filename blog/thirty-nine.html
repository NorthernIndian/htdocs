Thirty Nine

I'm officially on the back nine of life now. I've lived roughly half of my expected lifespan. I'm in the best shape of my life, but my lower back hurts sometimes. Grey hair is showing up everywhere. I find myself shaking my head at young people and spending more time with an older crowd. And I have a hernia.

Every year on my birthday, I like to look back and take stock. Here's what stood out in year thirty-nine.

Snapshot of the Year

Top 3 Memories

  • Spending time with family in Puerto Vallarta
  • Moose hunting
  • Running my first 50k ultra marathon in Montana

Stats at a Glance

  • ⛰️ 37,690 m elevation gain
  • ✈️ 52 flights taken
  • 🍺 229 alcoholic drinks
  • 📚 9 books read

3 Things That Worked

  • AI Training → amazing customized workouts

3 Things That Didn’t

  • Writing consistency → need to make time
  • No stretching or physio routine → still flirting with injury
  • Reading volume was the same → only going to read what peaks my interest from now on

✈️ Travel

Another year with a lot of travel: Whitefish, Guelph, Kincardine, Czar, Puerto Vallarta, and the ever-glamorous Fort Hills. In total, I logged 52 flights and traveled 48,602 km.

The big takeaway: We're getting really good at this travel thing.

🏃 Health and Running

I started using ChatGPT as my running coach in November and it has been amazing! I love the customized workouts and analyzing the data. It helped me take 10 minutes off my marathon time and I think there is still room for improvement.

I ran 3,320 km with 37,690 meters of elevation. That’s roughly 500k more than last year’s mileage. I completed four races, including my first ultra-marathon, and I’m proud to say I’ve maintained a two year run streak (and counting).

This year I improved my fueling knowledge when it comes to in-race nutrition. Thanks to David Roche, I learned that I can handle far more carbs during races than I ever thought possible. It's a massive confidence boost.

I think it's time to start chasing a sub-three-hour marathon.

I started a run club with one of my running buddies and it's been a learning experience with the social media stuff. It has made me very uncomfortable at times, but I think it has been an overall positive experience. I'm curious to see where this will go.

It has been difficult to maintain a stretching habit because I've rarely had to deal with injury until recently. I need to figure out how to make stretching a habit that sticks.

I hit the gym 73 times this year. I discovered that strength training twice a week is the perfect amount for me. I plan to keep this habit going for as long as possible.

❤️ Relationships

Marriage meetings and date nights have dropped a bit in the past several months, but I plan to get this back to a regular routine.

My goal with my kids remains the same: spend as much time with them as possible. I also want them to learn how to defend themselves, whether that's through martial arts or another discipline.

The only drawback to cutting back on alcohol is that you spend less time with friends who drink. I noticed a major cutback in my friendships since cutting back on alcohol, but luckily, I've been making a lot of running friends who don't drink. It kinda balances out, but I do miss the shenanigans of my old relationships - just not the hangovers.

One of the many drawbacks to aging is seeing family and friends get old and sick. My Grandma is dealing with dementia and some friends have had their parents die. It's that age where these things happen.

💻 Coding

I've given up on pursuing coding as a career. AI has changed the landscape dramatically, and I'm no longer interested in making that transition. I will continue coding as a hobby because I do enjoy it.

My plan is to write more blogs for both my personal site and running club site.

🗣️ Toastmasters

I gave three speeches, two short of my goal.

I am on my way out of Toastmasters - I just don't have the time anymore.

🍺 Alcohol

I logged 229 alcoholic drinks— down over 100 from the year before.

I still love a good pint, but I save it for special occasions now. My sleep has been amazing and the savings are insane.

☕ Caffeine

I drank 2–3 cups of coffee every single day. The exact same as last year.

📚 Reading

Just 9 books this year—the same as last year.

I plan to read only what piques my interest going forward and I am going to do my best to keep detailed notes.

🎯 Goals for Year 40

  • Stretch daily
  • Pursue a sub-there-hour marathon
  • Schedule more date nights
  • Enroll the kids in martial arts
  • Grow Big Hill Runners
  • Publish more blog posts
  • Further reduce alcohol consumption
  • Read only books of interest and keep notes
