Training

  • Plan: Strava-McMillian Plan that's included with the Strava subscription. I only had 4 weeks of training between Whitefish and this race.
  • Shoes: Brooks Ghost 15s.
  • Watch: Garmin Vivo Activ 4.
  • Apparel: Nike Flex 7" shorts, OC running hat, Solomon Active Skin 8, and Oakley Holbrooks.
  • Supplements: Huma Gels and LMT.

Nothing new going into this training block. I tried to throw in more hills because the race course had more elevation.

Pre-race

  • Accomidations: Home. I live an hour away from the course.
  • Race Package: Pickup was in the basement of an old country hall at the horse race track - it was unique. The race shirt in this package was not great. Next time I will pick up my package on race day because it was a 2 hour round trip.
  • Dinner: Can't recall.
  • Sleep: Pretty good, 7-8 hours.
  • Breakfast: Eggs, oatmeal, and a banana.
  • Travel to the Start Line: 1 hour drive to the race track and then a 20 minute shuttle to the start line at the high school.
  • Strategy: I didn't have a plan for this race. I just wanted to set a new PR.

Had to get up early for this one because of the travel. I was one of the first people to arrive at the race grounds and I caught the first shuttle to the start line. I was very early, so I just stood around and stretched. 15 minutes before the race, the PA fired up and blasted ZZ Top. A gentleman took the microphone to make an announcement but it was barely audible. The ZZ Top continued to play until 2 minutes before gun time. I don't recall the national anthem playing.

Race

  • Start Time: 08:00 AM
  • Shoes: Brooks Ghost 15s.
  • Watch: Garmin Vivo Activ 4.
  • Apparel: Nike Flex 7" shorts, Whitefish race t-shirt, OC running hat, Solomon Active Skin 8, and Oakley Holbrooks.
  • Supplements: (3) Huma Gels and LMT.
  • Hydration: (2) 500ml flasks. Overkill, I know.
  • Aid Stations: Did not use the aid stations because I wore my hydration vest.
  • Potty Breaks: None and no tummy trouble - thank god.

The race started with all distances at once so it was tough to tell who you were running beside. The course started downtown and looped on country roads. I thought it was a beautiful scenic course. I am definitely going to return for the full marathon.

Post-race

  • Finish Time: 01:38:32
  • Average Pace: 4:41/km
  • Overall Placement: 38th of 526
  • Category Placement: 6th of 35
  • Recovery Meal: Massive burger and fries at Mudmans.

This was the first race where I felt very strong. I was passing people throughout the race. I didn't expect my quads to get completely destroyed. One thing that really surprised me was the negative splits at the end of the race. I always struggled with ending races but this one was different, it felt like I was sprinting for the last few kilometers.

I also wasn't sure what my finish time was going to be. I assumed it was going to be tight to 01:44. When I saw 01:38 at the finish line, I was shocked. It was hard to believe that I beat my PR by six minutes. I was gleeful for several weeks after the race. It was a major confidence booster too.
