Training

I was training 4-5 days a week and averaging 55KM per week. All of my runs were based on pace.

My training was based on a 12-week Strava plan. I enjoyed it because I was able to program it into a google sheet and adjust the schedule as needed.

I bought a hydration vest during this training cycle and used it on my long runs. It was fantastic compared to the hydration belt.

I have a sensitive stomach and have struggled with supplementation in the past, but I settled on Huma gels and LMT hydration. There were zero side effects when I tested these supplements in training.

I trained exclusively in Brooks Ghost 15s, Nike Flex 7" shorts, and an OC running hat. My hydration vest was a Solomon Active Skin 8 and I wore Oakley Holbrooks.

I used a Garmin Vivo Activ 4 watch.

I received one massage per month leading up to the race.

My training block left me feeling confident before the race.

Pre-race

I woke up early. I ate eggs, oatmeal, and a banana. I had two coffees. Luckily I shat.

We left the condo 30 minutes early, but half way to the town I realized that my bib was missing. We had to turn around and drive back. I got to the start line 5 minutes before gun time. I consumed a gel and placed myself mid-pack.

I made a last minute decision to wear my hydration vest because I had trained with it and was worried about switching things up. I filled one flask with water and the other with LMT.

Race

The race started at 08:30am mountain time.

The marathon, half marathon, and 10k started at the same time, so it was tough to know who you were running with.

I decided to take it easy for the first bit. The course was beautiful and I really tried to soak it in.

Gel consumed at 9:15am.

As I was running, I noticed the course was marked in miles. I had no idea what 21.1K was in miles, so I was slightly confused about what distance I had covered. I could have looked at my watch, but I didn't want to.

Gel consumed at 10:00am.

As we came back to familiar territory, I realized there was only 1-2 km remaining. I put the jets on and started to sprint. I passed 4 people in the last stretch including a guy in the last 100m. He definitely wasn't expecting it.

I crossed the finish line in 1 hour and 38 minutes. I beat my previous PR by 6 minutes.

During this race, one of my flasks had water and the other had LMT. I consumed a gel before the race and then i consumed another 2-3 gels, but I can't remember now.

During the race I felt great, although there was a bit of a mental struggle around the 16k mark.

Post-race

I was almost in disbelief that I set a new PB. It felt so good.

One thing I wasn't expecting was how sore my quads were.

Ate a massive american burger for dinner and felt much better.

I loved this race. I like that it was on the May long weekend. I like that it wasn't big. And I liked the course. It was easy to access and there wasn't a lot of traffic.

I probably will run it again. Might even try the full marathon.
