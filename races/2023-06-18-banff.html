Training

  • Plan: Running Coach Deon Flynn.
  • Shoes: Brooks Ghost 14s.
  • Watch: I did not own a watch at this time.
  • Apparel: Nike Flex 7" shorts, old running hat, and Oakleys.
  • Supplements: Nothing.

I was frustrated by my lack of progress despite all the running, so I decided to hire a coach—Deon, a former colleague who, coincidentally, was a certified running coach. He started sending me weekly workouts, and it didn’t take long for me to realize two things: I hadn’t been pushing myself nearly hard enough, and I’d been training all wrong.

Race

  • Start Time: 09:30 AM
  • Shoes: Brooks Ghost 14s.
  • Watch: Old school casio g-shock.
  • Apparel: Nike Flex 7" shorts, Banff race t-shirt, old running hat, and Oakleys.
  • Supplements: Nothing.
  • Hydration: Aid stations.
  • Aid Stations: I made the mistake of trying GU gels at the aid station.
  • Potty Breaks: Nope.

I was feeling surprisingly good for most of the race—cruising along, thinking I had it all figured out. Then I made the classic rookie mistake: I grabbed a GU gel at one of the aid stations near the end. Big mistake! My stomach went full rebellion mode, and I was suddenly teetering on the edge of throwing up mid-stride. I ended up walking a few times just to keep things down- I did not want to yak in front of a crowd. Dignity first, finish line second.

Besides feeling ill from the gel, I was happy to beat my friend and enjoy some beers afterwards.
