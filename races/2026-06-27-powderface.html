Training

  • Plan: Coach Chat GPT
  • Shoes: Brooks Cascadia and Catamount.
  • Watch: Garmin Forerunner 255.
  • Gear: Solomon Active Skin 8, and trekking poles.
  • Supplements: Huma Gels, Stinger Gels, and Skratch.

I tried building off the Whitefish Marathon with a lot of uphill treadmill work and trail time. I think my long runs could have been longer and with more intent.

Pre-race

  • Accomidations: Home.
  • Race Package: No shirt which kinda sucks.
  • Dinner: Spaghetti.
  • Sleep: Pretty good, 7-8 hours.
  • Breakfast: Eggs, bagel, and a banana.
  • Travel to the Start Line: 35 minutes.
  • Strategy: Take it easy for the first hour, fuel well, and race the second half.

I arrived at Station Flats extra early since parking was limited. I had plenty of time to stretch and relax prior to the start.

Race

  • Start Time: 08:00 AM
  • Shoes: Brooks Catamount.
  • Watch: Garmin Forerunner 255.
  • Apparel: Nike Flex 7" shorts, old race shirt, Ceile hat, and Solomon Active Skin 8
  • Supplements: (9) Huma and Stinger Gels with Skratch.
  • Hydration: (5) 500ml flasks all filled with Skratch.
  • Aid Stations: Only used the aid stations for water.
  • Potty Breaks: The 23k aid station did not have a porta potty, so I had to squat over a log in the forest.

I was very excited for the race, it had been on my mind for months. I thought that I had a chance to make the podium if things went well. I was also excited to compete with my friend Dave Snively.

The race started with some light rain which was very nice as I like it cool. Dave took off like a rocket and so did a few other guys. Adrenaline got the better of me and I too pushed a bit. I knew this wasn't smart because my heart rate was way higher than I wanted it to be.

I ended up running with another guy up to the Powderface ridge which wasn't smart because he was pushing the pace and I was going a bit faster than I wanted too. I ended up slowing down and letting him go, but later I passed him on the downhill section.

When heading down Powderface, the jarring motion upset my stomach and I knew that I would have to shit sooner or later. I decided that it would be at the 23k aid station. Unfortunately, there wasn't a porta potty at the aid station, so I ran 200m past the aid station and pooped in the bush. Thank God I brought toilet paper.

The section between the 23k and 32k aid station was quiet and daunting. I didn't see another soul.

I looked at my watch around 28k and realized the I had been running for 3 hours and 15 minutes - the same time it took me to run the road marathon a month earlier. At this point I knew I was in for some pain.

I started struggling with gels and hydration after 30k - I believe I was behind on hydration and it was affecting how the gels were going down.

At the 32k air station, I got some plain water and chugged it. I knew that I was dehydrated based on how thirsty I was.

Post-race

  • Finish Time: 02:22:02
  • Average Pace: 6:18/km
  • Overall Placement: 6th of 77
  • Category Placement: 1st of 7
  • Recovery Meal: Can't recall.

I stuck around for a bit to see who made the podium. I thought there was a chance that I was 2nd or 3rd, but it turns out I was 4th in the men's category.

I would love to do this race again, but it was expensive and it wasn't a fun place for my family to hang out. The course was great though and I really enjoyed it. This would be a fun one to do again with friends.

It was also a great introduction to trail running.
