Training

  • Plan: This race fell on week 10 of 16 for my Whitefish 50k training.
  • Equipment: Solomon Active Skin 8, Black Diamond Distance FLZ Carbon Trekking Poles, Garmin Forerunner 255s, and a mix of shoes (Brooks Catamount, Novablast 3, New Balance Rebel v4, Saucony Endorphin Speed 4).
  • Supplements: Huma Gels, Electrolyte Pills, & LMT
  • Total Training Milage: 740 km
  • Total Training Elevation: 2487 m
  • VO2 Max: 53.3 ml/kg/min

I created a 16 week plan for the Whitefish 50k using chatGPT. I factored this race into the training program and I treated it like a long run, but I also went into with an experimental mindset. I wanted to see how my body reacted to the distance when laid back in zone 2. Would I have energy at the end? How would I feel the next day? Would it be less painful?

Either way I felt primed for the race.

Pre-race

  • Accommodations: Home.
  • Race Package: Pickup was at Born Brewing. They has sweet swag this year: a really nice Ceile hat and a pint glass with the race logo.
  • Dinner: Spahgetti.
  • Sleep: Pretty good, 7-8 hours.
  • Breakfast: Coffee, LMT, eggs, bagel with jam, and a cliff bar.
  • Travel to the Start Line: 30 minutes.
  • Strategy: High zone 2/low zone 3 for the first 30k and then reassess.

Like last year, I indulged in a couple tall boys from Born Brewing where I picked up my race package. Well, I did it again. It was light beer so I don't think it had any effect on my sleep or the race.

Race

  • Start Time: 08:30 AM
  • Shoes: Brooks Catamount 3.
  • Watch: Garmin Forerunner 255s.
  • Apparel: Nike Flex 7" shorts, old Banff race t-shirt, Ciele running hat, Solomon Active Skin 8, and Oakley Frogskins.
  • Supplements: Huma gels and electrolyte pills.
  • Hydration: I went through 5-6 liters of water.
  • Aid Stations: Only stopped for water.
  • Potty Breaks: Nope, but I had tummy trouble.

It was a perfect race day. The weather was cool and I felt great. I ran into Matt Labine at the start line which I thought was kinda funny - we had run together at Powderface the year before and chatted for a bit. It was nice to see a familiar face.

It started at 08:30am and I watched my heart rate the entire race. I slowed down whenever I noticed myself creeping into zone 3. I felt like I could have pushed a bit harder, but I was weary - I did not want to hit a wall this race.

I was surprisingly able to pass people at my zone 2 pace for the first 12-15k. There were a few people who passed me on the vertical sections, but I kept my eye on them.

As I approached the summit of Moose Mountain, I was surprised to see Mr Labine already descending - he was moving so fast and it impressed the hell out of me.

On the descent, I put my poles away and started sprinting down the mountain. I passed so many people, including the people that passed me earlier. I did not see any racers after Moose Packers for at least an hour or more.

It was around the 35k mark that the leader in the 50k passed me. I then started to see other runners ahead of me and I passed one of them, but he got by me again and took the 10th overall spot.

It was around the same time that I started to feel nauseous and the gels were starting to make me feel sick. I thought I was going to vomit a few times.

But I kept a steady pace despite the troubles and I finished strong. It was nice to see my family and Mark S.at the finish line.

Post-race

  • Finish Time: 04:54:44
  • Average Pace: 7:12/km
  • Overall Placement: 11th of 53
  • Category Placement: 6th of 16
  • Recovery Meal: Mucho Burrito - the biggest burrito they had.

I ended up staying in zone 2 for 90% of the race which makes me think that I should have pushed a little harder on the easy sections, but this is the first race I really enjoyed from start to finish.

Learning my lesson from Vancouver and this race, I now understand that I have to both monitor my heart rate and figure out my in-race nutrition. I can't survive on gels alone - real food is absolutely necessary to be successful in the future. So from here on out I will start experimenting with different foods while running.
